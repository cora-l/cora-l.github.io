Enjoy the smooth , rich favour delicious egg tart with cookie like shell!

Ingredients
for 14-16 servings of egg tart pan at 7cm diameter

Preparation time : 30mins
Cooking time: 20-25mins

CUSTARD FILLING

  • 4 eggs, beaten, reserve 2 tablespoons for your pastry dough
  • 1 cup water 180ml, hot
  • 6 tbsp sugar

PASTRY DOUGH

  • 2 cups cake flour (200 g), plus extra for dusting
  • 115 g unsalted butter (115 g), room temperature
  • Â¼ cup powdered sugar (40 g)
  • 2 tablespoons egg, beaten
  • â…› teaspoon salt
  • Â¼ tablespoons vanilla oil and coconut oil , optional
    *may add more if dough is too dry after fridge

Preparation


For the pastry,

  1. Sift flour, sugar, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Add softened butter.
    Bring the mixture together with your hands, careful not to knead the pastry dough too much or you will make the pastry tough.
  3. Whisk the egg yolks and add the 2 tablespoons of beaten yolk to the flour mixture. Bring together until smooth.
    If the dough is too sticky, coating your hands with flour will help.
  4. Cover with plastic wrap and then refrigerate for 30 minutes, or until the dough is firm.
    For the custard filling,
  5. Melt sugar and salt with hot water.
  6. Mix until dissolved then let cool.
  7. Add the rest of the beaten egg yolk. Stir in sugar water and also evaporated milk (if adding vanilla, add now).
  8. Stir and combine everything well.
  9. Strain the filling to ensure no lumps.
  10. Preheat the oven to 400ËšF (200ËšC.)
  11. Take the dough out and divide into 16 equal portions or about 7-8gm each portion.
  12. Spray the tart pan with a light coating of oil.
  13. Take one portion of your dough and roll it into a ball and place in your tart shell. Press the shell into the pan with your thumb and two fingers to press the edge into uniform thickness.
    Watch how the egg tart master did in the below video!
  1. Pour the custard filling into the shells until it is about 80% full.
  2. Put the tart into lower shelf of the oven
  3. Bake for 3-5mins with both upper and lower heat till the mustard filling is formed in nice shape then switch to lower heat for the rest 15 to 20 minutes until the surface becomes golden brown and a toothpick can stand in the egg tart.
  4. Cool down on a rack for several minutes and then take the egg tarts out.
    Enjoy !

TIPs

  1. Make sure to use fresh egg , the color of filling is more bright yellow
  2. Make sure the mixture of the filling are dissolved or just silt before pouring into the shell
  3. Can press the dough to 4mm thickness and precut the dough by a cup or the egg tart shell so as to get a uniform thickness.
  4. Evaporated milk gives a silky taste to the egg tart
  5. Constant check the shape and color of the filling and shell during baking.
